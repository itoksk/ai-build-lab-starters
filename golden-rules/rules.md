# The 7 golden rules of vibe coding

Seven habits for building with AI — the line between a frustrating session and a smooth one. They aren't to memorize; they're a cheat-sheet for when you get stuck. Verbatim from the Skill page.

> Live: https://ai-build-lab.pages.dev/skills/seven-golden-rules

1. **No code first** — open with a planning chat: "interview me, don't code yet."
2. **Step by step** — one working piece, confirm, then add the next.
3. **Be specific** — name the thing and the change: "bigger blue button."
4. **Ask for logs** — "show the progress on screen" makes bugs findable.
5. **Expect weird input** — decide up front what happens on nonsense input.
6. **Settings in one place** — group changeable numbers and words together.
7. **Safe to press twice** — double-clicking a button never breaks anything.

## Why it matters

Without them the AI builds the wrong thing all at once, breaks five stacked changes, and corrupts data on a double-click. Follow the seven and the whole session runs smooth.

## One change per message

The core skill underneath all seven: change ONE thing per message, confirm it works, then the next. Stack five changes and break it, and you won't know which one did it. "Do everything at once" feels fast and ends in a tangle you can't fix.
